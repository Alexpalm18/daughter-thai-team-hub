Server Training Guide

Steps of Service & Table Approach — Daughter Thai Kitchen

← Back to Staff Hub

At Daughter Thai Kitchen, our service style is warm, attentive, genuine, and efficient. We want every guest to feel welcomed, guided, and taken care of from the moment they sit down.


The goal is not just to take orders — it is to create a comfortable and organized dining experience.

Steps of Service
1
Greeting the Table

Approach the table within 1–2 minutes after guests are seated. Smile, make eye contact, and greet confidently.

Standard Greeting
"Hi, welcome to Daughter Thai Kitchen. My name is Joy, and I'll be taking care of you guys today."
2
Ask if It's Their First Time

This helps you gauge how much guidance the guests may need.

Ask
"Is this your first time dining with us?"
✓ First Time

"Well, welcome to Daughter Thai! Thank you so much for being here today." Offer to guide them through the menu.

↩ Returning Guest

"Welcome back! Thank you so much for coming back to see us."

3
Offer Water
Ask
"Can we start you off with water? We have sparkling mineral water or regular tap water."
⚡
Important Bring water promptly. This is part of the guest's first impression — water service should feel quick and attentive.
4
Return for Drinks & Appetizers

After dropping off water, return and ask for other drinks.

Ask
"Can I get you started with any cocktails, wine, beer, or other drinks today?"
If They Need More Time
"No problem at all, take your time. I'll come back for drinks and appetizers."
💡
Why This Matters This guides the natural flow of service. Guests understand the next step is drinks and appetizers — they won't feel rushed or lost in the menu.
5
Ask About Allergies Early

Before taking food orders, always ask about allergies or dietary restrictions.

Ask
"Before we order, does anyone at the table have any allergies or dietary restrictions I should know about?"
Gluten-Free Example
"Anything marked GFO on the menu means it can be made gluten-free."
⚠️
Important Never guess about allergies. Always check with the kitchen or manager if unsure. Guest safety comes first.
6
Guide Guests Through Our Spice Levels

Our spice scale is based on Thai standards — which are significantly hotter than most Thai restaurants in the United States. Always set this expectation clearly when guests order spicy dishes.

Key Message for Guests Our "medium" is not the same as a typical American Thai restaurant's medium. Always use the number scale — it helps guests understand immediately. "Our Thai Medium is about a 6 or 7 out of 10."
How to Communicate It
"Just so you know, our spice levels are based on Thai standards — they're a bit hotter than most Thai places here. Our Thai Medium is about a 6 or 7 out of 10. Would you like to go with that, or would you prefer Thai Mild to start?"
7
Taking the Food Order

When returning to take the food order, never approach with "Are you ready?" Instead, use a warm, team-oriented tone.

✓ Say This

"Do we have any questions about the menu?"
or
"Are we ready to order?"

✕ Not This

"Are you ready?"

💬
Why Using "we" feels warmer, smoother, and less abrupt — it keeps the tone collaborative and hospitable.
8
After Taking the Order

Before leaving the table, let guests know what happens next so they feel informed and at ease.

Say
"Perfect, I'll get these started for you, and I'll be back if you have any questions."
9
Confirm Orders Were Sent Correctly

After entering the order, always verify it went through properly.

  • ✓ Double check every item
  • ✓ Confirm modifiers are correct
  • ✓ Make sure the ticket reached the kitchen
⚠️
Important Handheld devices and POS systems can fail or disconnect. Always verify the kitchen received the ticket.
10
Table Check-Backs VERY IMPORTANT

Servers should check back at least 3 times during the meal.

1st Check

Right After Food Arrives

"Does everything look okay here? Can I grab you extra napkins, chili oil, spice tray, or chopsticks?"

2nd Check

Mid-Meal

"How is everything tasting so far?" — Catch issues early before the guest leaves unhappy.

3rd Check

End of Meal

"How was everything tonight?" Then transition: "Would you like to look at dessert or another round of drinks?"

💡
Why Check-Backs Matter Catching issues early prevents bad experiences, negative reviews, and uncomfortable situations at the end of service.
High-Alert Dishes & Drinks — Special Warnings Required
ℹ️
Why This Section Exists Some dishes require extra communication before the order is placed. Taking a few extra seconds to warn the guest protects their experience — and yours. Once the food is served, we cannot take it back.
🥗
Papaya Salad
Starts at Thai Medium — approximately 6–7 out of 10

This dish starts at Thai Medium — which is already a 6–7 on our scale. Many guests assume "medium" means mild. Always clarify before the order is finalized.

What to Say
"Just so you know, our papaya salad starts at Thai Medium — that's about a 6 or 7 out of 10 on our spice scale. Would you like it at that level, or would you prefer Thai Mild?"
🌶️
Always Give the Number Saying "6 or 7 out of 10" makes it click. Most guests will nod and say "oh okay" — that's a win. They're informed and can order with confidence.
🍛
Southern Fish Curry
Extreme heat · Distinct fermented fish flavor · Warn at least 3 times

This is one of our most authentically intense dishes and the one that catches guests off guard most often. It requires a specific, repeated warning — every single time.

Two things make this dish different:

  • 🔥 Extreme spice — this is 12 out of 10. Not a typo.
  • 🐟 Distinct fermented fishiness — we use fermented fish guts in the curry. It is a non-coconut milk curry with a bold, pungent flavor that is not for everyone.
⚠ Warn at Least 3 Times
  1. 1
    Warn about the heat. "This curry is extremely spicy — I'd say 12 out of 10. It's one of the hottest dishes we serve."
  2. 2
    Warn about the flavor profile. "It's also not a typical curry — there's no coconut milk, and we use fermented fish, so it has a very distinct, bold fishiness to it."
  3. 3
    Confirm they still want it. "We just want to make sure it's something you'll really enjoy. Are you comfortable with both the heat and the flavor?"
💛
If the Guest Gets Irritated by the Warnings Some guests may feel you're being too cautious. Reassure them warmly — it's not about stopping them, it's about making sure they love what they ordered.

"I'm sorry — I don't mean to be repetitive! We absolutely love this dish and want you to try it. We just want to make sure it's truly something you'll enjoy, because we care about your experience."
🚫 No Returns Policy

We do not take this dish back if it turns out to be too spicy or not to the guest's taste. Once you have warned them thoroughly and they order, you can confidently hold that line. Your job is to warn — and you did.

✅ If They're Struggling — Be Helpful

Even after warnings, if a guest is having difficulty with the heat, step in with care. Offer a glass of milk or sliced cucumber to help cool things down. We can't take the dish back, but we can still take care of the guest.

🍹
Muay Thai Punch — Cocktail
Bold ginger · Bitters · Tamarind — not a typical sweet cocktail

The Muay Thai Punch is made with vodka, elderflower liqueur, ginger, and tamarind — and it is sweet and fruity. The surprise for most guests comes from the tamarind sauce, which adds a funky, complex depth that goes beyond what people expect from a cocktail. People who love it, love it. But guests who aren't ready for that character often want to swap it out.

Because this comes up often, always give guests a heads-up about the tamarind before they order. One sentence is all it takes.

What to Say
"The Muay Thai Punch is sweet and fruity — vodka, elderflower, ginger — but the tamarind sauce gives it a really unique, complex funkiness that some people love and some people find surprising. Just wanted to mention it so you know what to expect!"
🍹
Know Your Guest If a guest seems drawn to sweeter, more familiar cocktails, take a beat to describe the tamarind character. It's a small moment that prevents a big complaint later.
🚫 No Returns on Drinks

We cannot take the drink back if a guest doesn't enjoy the flavor — that is a cost the restaurant absorbs every time, and it adds up. Once you've described it and they order, the responsibility is theirs. You warned them, and that's what matters.

✅ If They're Unhappy — Help Them, But Be Clear

Acknowledge their feeling kindly: "I'm so sorry it wasn't quite what you expected!" Then offer to help them find something they'll enjoy from the menu. Do not replace the drink. They are still responsible for paying for the original cocktail — a new drink would be an additional order.
